Article Title: Rooftop Gardens Are Changing Cities Author: Maria Lopez
Published: March 15, 2026

Introduction: Urban farming is transforming unused city spaces into productive
green zones. Rooftop gardens are leading this change.

Section 1 Title: Why Rooftop Gardens Matter Content: Cities face food insecurity
and environmental stress. Rooftop gardens reduce heat and improve air quality.

Highlighted Note: Urban farms can reduce city temperatures by 2°C.

Data Point: Average yield per rooftop: 500 kg per year.

Section 2 Title: Real-World Example Content: In 2024, the SkyGrow Project
launched in New York City.

Image: Photo of a rooftop garden in NYC Caption: Volunteers planting vegetables
at SkyGrow Project.

Expandable Details: Project Funding: The initial investment was $250,000.
